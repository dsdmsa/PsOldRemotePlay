# Wikipedia — Remote Play (PS3 Sections)
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remote_Play

## History
Remote Play was officially revealed prior to the PS3's October 2006 launch. Sony demonstrated streaming PS3 output to a PSP during their "Gamer's Day" event — showing downloaded PlayStation games and movies being streamed.

Initially, Sony announced "all original PlayStation games would support the feature" but they had to be digital downloads. By end of 2007, a firmware update expanded compatibility to include disc-based original PlayStation games.

## Supported Devices
- **Primary:** PlayStation Portable (PSP)
- **Later:** PlayStation Vita, Sony Xperia smartphones/tablets, Sony VAIO desktops/laptops (April 2010 firmware update)

## Technical Capabilities
Two modes:
1. **Local Network:** Both devices on same home network with wireless transmission
2. **Remote:** Internet-based connection through Sony's servers

## Key Limitation
Despite early emphasis, Remote Play saw minimal adoption among PS3 games. Titles like Gran Turismo HD and Formula One Championship Edition had the feature removed before release. Most compatible titles were small PSN-only games, with Lair being a rare exception among physical Blu-ray releases.

**Note:** With custom firmware (webMAN-MOD), Remote Play can be forced to work with ALL PS3 games, not just officially supported ones.
